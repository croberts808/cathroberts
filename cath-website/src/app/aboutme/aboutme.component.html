I am a UX Designer. I study Computer Science, Mathematics, and Cognitive Science at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

Questions you may have for me:

Why UX Design? As a computer science major, I took for granted the fact that I would become a software engineer. After all, that's what most of my classmates are doing, so I should do that too, right? When I learned about the field of User Experience, however, I saw a potential to fulfill multiple passions at once. I love psychology, coding, creating art, and most importantly, helping people by taking away their struggles.

Why math? Throughout middle school and high school, I loved spending hours a day on complex math challenges. I felt most comfortable in math club, where I did not feel judged for enjoying probability, competing at math contests all over the state, and memorizing the digits of pi. While I primarily love design and coding, I want to always keep my passion for numbers and finding beautiful solutions alive.

What are my favorite creative outlets? I love to crochet, knit, embroider, create digital art, design applications on Figma, and make paper crafts.
